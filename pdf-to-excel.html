How to Convert PDF to Excel

Converting PDF documents to Excel is essential when you need to:

  • Extract financial data from statements or reports
  • Convert tables from PDFs into editable spreadsheets
  • Analyze data that's locked in PDF format
  • Create databases from printed or scanned documents

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Upload your PDF file - We support both text-based and scanned PDFs

2. Set extraction options - Choose whether to extract tables, text, or use OCR

3. Convert to Excel - Process takes just seconds for most documents

4. Download your spreadsheet - Get perfectly structured data every time

Pro Tips

  • Use "Extract tables" for documents with clear tabular data
  • Enable OCR for scanned documents or image-based PDFs
  • For complex layouts, try both table and text extraction
  • Check data formatting after conversion and adjust as needed

Frequently Asked Questions

What types of PDFs can be converted to Excel?

We support both text-based PDFs (created from Word, Excel, etc.) and scanned/image-based PDFs (with OCR option enabled).

How accurate is the table extraction?

Our converter achieves over 95% accuracy for well-structured PDF tables. Complex layouts may require minor adjustments after conversion.

Is there a file size limit?

You can convert files up to 50MB for free. Our premium version handles files up to 200MB with faster processing.

How secure is my document?

All files are automatically deleted from our servers after processing. We never store or access your documents after conversion.

Can I convert Excel back to PDF?

Yes! Try our Excel to PDF tool for high-quality document conversion.
