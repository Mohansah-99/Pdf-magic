How to Convert PDF to Word

Converting PDF to Word is essential when you need to:

  • Edit content from PDF files
  • Reuse text from PDF documents
  • Update old PDF forms or documents
  • Extract content for repurposing

Step-by-Step Guide

1. Upload your PDF file - We support text-based and scanned PDFs (with OCR)

2. Set conversion options - Choose output format and layout preferences

3. Convert to Word - Process takes just seconds for most documents

4. Download your Word file - Get editable document with preserved formatting

Pro Tips

  • Use ".docx" format for best compatibility with modern Word versions
  • Enable OCR for scanned documents or image-based PDFs
  • For complex layouts, check "Preserve original layout" option
  • After conversion, review formatting and adjust as needed in Word

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the PDF to Word conversion?

Our converter achieves 95%+ accuracy for text-based PDFs with clear formatting. Complex layouts may require minor adjustments in Word after conversion.

Can I convert scanned PDFs to Word?

Yes! Enable the OCR (Optical Character Recognition) option to convert scanned documents or image-based PDFs to editable Word files.

Will tables and images be preserved?

Yes, our converter preserves tables, images, and most formatting elements. For best results with complex tables, use the "Preserve layout" option.

Is there a file size limit?

The free version supports PDF files up to 50MB. For larger files, consider our premium service which handles documents up to 200MB.

Can I convert Word back to PDF?

Yes! Try our Word to PDF tool to convert your edited document back to PDF format.
